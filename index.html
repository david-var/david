Migrating to Apollo iOS 2.0: A Team Journey

December 2024

Our iOS team recently completed a significant migration from Apollo iOS 1.x to version 2.0.3. This was a breaking change that required careful planning and execution. I had the opportunity to provide technical guidance and code reviews throughout the process, working alongside a talented engineer who drove the implementation.

Why we upgraded:

  • Swift 6 & Modern Concurrency — Apollo 2.0 was completely reimplemented using async/await and structured concurrency, replacing the old callback-based APIs
  • Improved Thread Safety — Most types now conform to Sendable, making our codebase more robust in concurrent scenarios
  • Future-proofing — CocoaPods support was dropped in 2.0, so staying on 1.x would have meant falling behind on critical updates
  • Better Type Safety — Streamlined APIs with clearer separation of concerns and more precise typing

The migration involved updating our minimum deployment target to iOS 15+, refactoring custom interceptors to the new framework, and incrementally moving from callbacks to async/await patterns. It was a great example of how thorough code reviews and collaborative problem-solving can help a team navigate a complex upgrade smoothly.
